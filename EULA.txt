6. TELEMETRY (send_reports)

Verité is an evolving project. We already have plans for future modules and
features, and are pre-emptively addressing one flag already mentioned in the
preset settings.yml. "send_reports" is a setting that, if enabled, will send
reports of Verité's actions directly to teacommontea. This is an optional flag
that is disabled by default, and is only intended to allow us to gather data on
how the software is behaving on servers.

Up to, and not exceeding the following, we may collect data on:
- The number of times each module is triggered, and the actions taken by the
  software.
- The types of actions taken by the software, and the contexts in which they
  occur.
- The frequency and distribution of different types of abuse or unwanted actions
  that the software is designed to address.
- The performance and resource usage of the software on different servers and
  configurations.
- The effectiveness of the software in preventing abuse and unwanted actions, as
  measured by the reduction in such incidents on servers that use it.
- Global player movement data, up to 20 seconds before and after a module is
  triggered.
- The types of players involved in incidents that trigger the software, such as
  their playtime, client, version, and other non-identifying information.

Your data, if send_reports is enabled, is anonymized and aggregated, and will
not be used to identify you or your server. We will not use this data for any
purpose other than improving the Verité Suite. Your data will not be sold,
shared, or used for any other purpose.

We anonymize and aggregate this data in the following ways:
- We do not collect UUIDs.
- We do not collect IP addresses.
- We do not collect player names.
- We do not collect server names or locations.
- We do not collect files or logs from your server.
- We do not collect any other information that could be used to identify you or
  your server.

We want to know what happened, not who was involved.

The maximum extent of what we allow ourselves to collect:
- Block locations
- Player locations
- Player gamemodes
- Player inventories
- Player actions
- Player interactions
- Player movement data
- Player client and version data
- Player playtime data
- Module trigger data
- Module action data
- Module context data

If at any time we decide to collect additional data, we will only do so in a way
that requires a re-download of the software, and we will make reasonable efforts
to disclose these changes in future EULAs, release notes, and on GitHub.
